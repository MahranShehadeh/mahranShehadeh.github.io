About

An unconventional path to biophysics.

I am a final‑year PhD researcher at the Weizmann Institute of Science. My background is unconventional for a biologist: I started in High‑Energy Physics (ATLAS / CERN). There, I learned to handle massive datasets and hunt for rare events with extreme statistical rigor.

Today, I apply those same principles to the photon‑starved regime of single‑molecule optics. I am passionate about reproducible analysis, open‑source code, and clear scientific communication.

Whether it's a particle collision or a single fluorescing molecule, the instinct is the same: build the instrument, gather the data carefully, and let statistics — not wishful thinking — tell the story.
