Research

Watching single molecules, one photon at a time.

My work sits at the intersection of optics, nanofabrication, and machine learning. The goal: push single‑molecule fluorescence into regimes — higher concentrations, faster dynamics, noisier data — where it was never meant to work, and then make sense of what we see.

Research Highlights

Plasmon‑Enhanced FRET

Overcoming concentration barriers in single‑molecule fluorescence. I design gold nanoantennas (ZMWs / nanorods) that act as "amplifiers" for light, enhancing photon yield (~10×) and enabling microsecond temporal resolution.

Nanofabrication Optics

Deep Learning Analysis

Photon streams are noisy. I develop custom pipelines using LSTMs and Bayesian inference to robustly identify protein states from low‑SNR data, significantly outperforming standard HMMs.

Python / PyTorch Bayesian Inference

FDTD & Simulation

Before building, I simulate. Using FDTD (Lumerical) and Boundary Element Methods, I model electromagnetic fields to optimize antenna geometries for maximum enhancement and minimal quenching.

FDTD Maxwell Solvers

How it fits together

① Design & simulate

Model the electromagnetic field around candidate nanoantenna geometries to predict enhancement, quenching, and field confinement — before committing to fabrication.

② Fabricate & measure

Build the optimized structures and run single‑molecule fluorescence / FRET experiments on custom optical setups, collecting photon‑resolved time traces.

③ Analyze with AI

Feed low‑SNR photon streams through deep‑learning and Bayesian pipelines to extract conformational states and kinetics that classical methods miss.

④ Iterate

Close the loop: what the data reveals feeds back into the next generation of antenna designs and analysis models.
