About Me

I am currently completing my Ph.D. at the Weizmann Institute of Science under Prof. Gilad Haran.

My background is unconventional for a biologist: I started in High-Energy Physics (ATLAS/CERN). There, I learned to handle massive datasets and hunt for rare events with extreme statistical rigor.

Today, I apply those same principles to the photon-starved regime of single-molecule optics. I build custom plasmonic nanoantennas to enhance signals and develop Deep Learning (LSTM) & Bayesian pipelines to decode protein dynamics that are invisible to standard analysis.

Research Focus

Plasmon-Enhanced FRET

Overcoming the concentration barrier in single-molecule fluorescence. I design and fabricate plasmonic nanoantennas (gold nanorods/ZMWs) that act as "amplifiers" for light, enhancing photon yield and enabling microsecond temporal resolution.

Nanofabrication (FIB/EBL) Optics Fluorescence

Deep Learning Analysis

Photon streams are noisy. I develop custom computational pipelines using LSTMs (Long Short-Term Memory networks) and Bayesian inference to robustly identify structural states in proteins from low-SNR data, outperforming traditional HMMs.

Python/PyTorch Bayesian Statistics Time-Series Analysis

Optical Simulation (FDTD)

Before building, I simulate. Using FDTD (Finite-Difference Time-Domain) and Boundary Element Methods, I model electromagnetic fields to optimize antenna geometries for maximum fluorescence enhancement and minimal quenching.

Lumerical FDTD Maxwell's Equations Simulation
